Step 1

* **Input:** Identify identical or near-square numbers
* **Output:** Construct an intermediate value by multiplication that approximates a square
* **Execution:**

  * If two numbers are identical → compute `num1 * num2` such as `13 * 13 = 169`
  * If no identical pair → choose two numbers whose product or ratio is close to a perfect square such as `3 * 8 = 24`, close to 25
  * Only **one multiplication** is allowed here; do **not** square a computed result `(expr)*(expr)`

---

Step 2

* **Input:** Step 1 result + remaining numbers
* **Output:** Combine results to approach 24
* **Execution:**

  * Use remaining two numbers with `+`, `-`, `*`, or `/`
  * Try simple forms like `(step1_result - num3) / num4`, `(step1_result + num3) / num4`, `(step1_result / num3) + num4`, or `(step1_result / num3) - num4`
  * Use parentheses as needed to control order; each original number must appear **exactly once**

---

Step 3

* **Input:** Final result
* **Output:** Check if equals 24
* **Execution:**

  * If result == 24 → success
  * Else → retry Step 1 with a different pair or operator combination
