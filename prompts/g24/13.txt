Step 1 — Assign Roles
*Input: Four numbers `[a, b, c, d]`
*Output: Construct an intermediate product from two numbers
*Execution:
1. Enumerate all possible pairs of two numbers `(x, y)` from `[a, b, c, d]`.
2. For each pair, compute `x * y`. This is your intermediate product. **Do not skip any pair**.

Step 2 — First Adjustment*
*Input:Intermediate product and one remaining number `z`
*Output:Adjust the product toward 24 using addition or subtraction
*Execution:
1. For the remaining numbers, try **both addition and subtraction** with the intermediate product: `(x*y) + z`, `(x*y) - z`, `z + (x*y)`, `z - (x*y)`.
2. **Do not skip any order** — every permutation must be considered.

Step 3 — Final Operation*
*Input: Result from Step 2 and the last remaining number `w`
*Output: Reach 24 using final operation
*Execution:
1. Try all four operations: `(Step2_result + w)`, `(Step2_result - w)`, `(Step2_result * w)`, `(Step2_result / w)`.
2. If any of these equals 24, output the solution.

Step 4 — Validation and Backtracking**
*Input: All combinations tried
*Output: Final validation
*Execution:
1. If 24 is not reached, **backtrack**: try a different pair in Step 1, or a different operation/order in Step 2.
2. Repeat until all combinations are exhausted or a solution is found.
