Step 1

* Input: Four numbers `[a, b, c, d]`
* Output: Identify candidates for a “base” number to multiply by a fraction
* Execution: Choose a number `x` as the multiplier. Among the remaining numbers `[y, z, w]`, check if one can form a fraction `(y ± z / w)` or `(y ± w / z)` such that `fraction * x = 24`.

Step 2

* Input: Chosen multiplier `x` and fraction candidate `(y ± z / w)`
* Output: Construct the expression `x * (y ± z / w)` or `(y ± z / w) * x`
* Execution: Compute `x * (y ± z / w)` for all permutations of `[y, z, w]` and both `+`/`-`.

Step 3

* Input: Result of Step 2
* Output: Check if equals 24
* Execution:

  * If result = 24 → success
  * Else → try different permutation of `[y, z, w]` or switch `+`/`-` in fraction
  * Repeat until all permutations exhausted

Step 4 (optional small adjustments)

* Input: Expression that is close to 24 but not exact
* Output: Minor corrections using leftover numbers
* Execution: Incorporate remaining numbers as small additive/subtractive adjustments, e.g., `(x * (y ± z / w)) ± leftover`
